The Home Stretch

3/28/2016
It does not matter how slowly you go so long as you do not stop. -- Confucius

One month left. That's it. 30 days from now I will be standing in front of potential employers trying to convince them to hire me for a full-time programming job.

It seems like a million years ago that I stepped into that packed Rutgers classroom and started relearning the basics of HTML. Partly because that was almost 6 months ago and partly because of the dizzying pace at which we've learned new concepts since then: Node, SQL, Express, Handlebars, Passport, MongoDB, and last, but definitely not easiest, AngularJS.

I can't say that I'm an expert at any of these topics yet, but I am confident that I at least have a solid, basic understanding of each one. The more that I think about it, the more I am convinced that's the best any of us can really hope for from going to school. You're never going to learn what you need to know for a job until you are actually doing it, and I am really excited to get started.

Here's to finishing strong, getting a handle on these last few concepts, and continuing the trek along this awesome new career path.

Halfway Through!

2/7/2016
“Anything worth doing is worth doing poorly--until you can learn to do it well.” ― Zig Ziglar

It has been over 3 full months since my first class in this wild ride of a bootcamp. As promised, I have had moments of brilliance, and, probably more often, moments where I felt like I would never understand this web development stuff even if I had all of the time in the world! It has been fun, and I can confidently say that I have learned much more than I ever could have by just studying this stuff by myself at home.

Way back when I was a guitar class T.A. in college, I used to tell the beginning students the above quote by Ziglar. This wasn't meant to discourage them, but to impress upon them the reality that they were probably going to suck at guitar for awhile...their fingers and wrists would hurt, their brains would feel like they were trying to read alphabet soup, and their roommates would probably ask them more than once if they had somewhere else to practice. But if they kept practicing, especially when they didn't feel like it, they would start to sound and feel like real guitarists.

I am definitely taking my own advice to heart now. Some days I feel like I can't remember anything I've learned and it's super discouraging, but when I take a step back I can see just how far I've come. I just built my own node web server for this portfolio...how freaking cool is that?! I didn't even know what that was a week ago! I'm super excited to see what happens when all of these lessons start to come together, but for now I just need to keep working on feeling and being a web developer.

Opening Thoughts...

10/30/2015
Every time you post something online, you have a choice. You can either make it something that adds to the happiness levels in the world - or you can make it something that takes away...There is enough sadness in the world already. You don't need to add to it. - Zoe Sugg

Welcome to the initial installment of my blog! I will try to use this space as an accurate recording of my web development victories and fails. Hopefully, I will be able to look back at these entries and realize just how far I was able to go in six short months.

As I write this, it is Fall of 2015, and my favorite team, the New York Mets, are down 3-1 in the World Series. Much like them, I sometimes feel like an underdog in life in general. On the surface, I probably don't look like a typical web developer, gamer, or classically trained musician, but the cool thing about being an underdog is that you get to surprise the heck out of people pretty often. I pray that my Mets are able to do that, but more importantly I hope that I can reach the goal that I have set for myself of finishing the Bootcamp and securing a job that I really enjoy.
